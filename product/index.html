YSDN2017 Kiosk Interface • 2017

.

For my year's grad show, I designed the layouts for touchscreen kiosks that would show both interactive and motion work to the Toronto design community, parents, and friends. Built on top of the Masonry JS library by my peers, I had to figure out an effective way of sorting student work while keeping it accessible for those who might not be able to reach the top of the touchscreen. Another limitation was that no keyboards could be used for data entry.

.

Home & Filter Views

From the home screen, users can sort by motion and interactive work or tap the pink button to find work by individual students. Browse through students faster with the letters on the right side.

.

After filtering by students, the user can tap on the thumbnail to access the project.

.

In Use

Thanks to Tiffany Peng and Luc Palombo for developing the final product.

Bleu Cooking App • 2016

.

As part of a design challenge, I was asked to design a cooking app in under a week. The only requirement was to have a timer that could be triggered while reading a recipe.

.

Feed & Profile Views

Like most social media apps, users can publish their own recipes, follow others, and like/save recipes.

.

Recipe View & In-Text Alarm

1. The timeline on the left scales vertically to full height as the user scrolls down through the recipe.

2. Change the number of people the recipe can serve by tapping on the respective icon.

3. Check off recipe ingredients while grocery shopping, and recipe instructions while cooking.

Demo Music Collaboration App • 2015

.

Made in 3rd year with Aline Nguyen, Demo is an proof-of-concept app that's intended for musicians to collaborate on their music remotely. Demo keeps logs of past versions of songs and allows band members to add annotations to sections they want changed. Reflecting back on it now, I'd probably change a few things like larger type size, larger buttons, condensing the first 2 screens into a dropdown folder structure, and more obvious iconography.
