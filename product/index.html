Collective Experiential Dance App • 2017

.

°°°° Synopsis

Good music and dance are key components in a successful party. A lack of engagement in the party can stem from the wrong music coupled with the fear of dancing. Collective is a proof-of-concept app which allows party-goers to have a say in the music that gets played and allows for them to interact with dynamic visuals.

.

°°°° Key Features

Understanding that music and dance are essential components in social gatherings, I examined applications in the realm of interactive dance. What I found was these dancing applications were limited by:

1. Focus on level completion
The aspect of level completion isn’t necessarily negative, but I think for the application to be successful in a party setting, it needs to be more casual and inviting. By eliminating the competitive aspect, anyone can join in at any time and the flow of music can be more continuous.

2. Music selection & discoverability
By allowing users to add songs from their library, the experience becomes more engaging and personal, while the added feature of voting democratizes it.

3. Social interaction
Simply put, people connect over music. To aid in the process, users get notified when others like their song (saving that song a personal list). Furthermore, it helps everyone at the party gauge what genres are popular.

.

°°°° Structure

.

°°°° User Flows

.

°°°° Join The Party

Joining a room is easy. Simply type in the 4-digit code provided by the host's computer. Let people know who you are by taking a quick pic of yourself.

.

°°°° Save The Songs You Love

If you like what you're hearing at the moment, tap on the heart icon to like/save a song. Tap on the home button in the lower left to access your liked songs. View them in the default music app, or add them to notes. People are notified when their songs are liked.

.

°°°° Find People With Similar Tastes

Tap on a user's icon to bring up a modal which shows all the previous songs they've added to the queue.

.

°°°° Vote On Songs

Nearing the end of each song, partygoers will have the ability to quickly vote on the songs they want to hear next. Once the time has ended, the most popular song is played. As admin, you can rearrange and songs, or remove them entirely.

YSDN2017 Kiosk Interface • 2017

.

°°°° Synopsis

For my year's grad show, I designed the layouts for touchscreen kiosks that would show both interactive and motion work to the Toronto design community, parents, and friends. Built on top of the Masonry JS library by my peers, I had to figure out an effective way of sorting student work while keeping it accessible for those who might not be able to reach the top of the touchscreen. Another limitation was that no keyboards could be used for data entry.

.

°°°° Home & Filter Views

From the home screen, users can sort by motion and interactive work or tap the pink button to find work by individual students. Browse through students faster with the letters on the right side.

.

After filtering by students, the user can tap on the thumbnail to access the project.

.

Demo Music Collaboration App • 2014

.

°°°° Synopsis

Made in 3rd year with Aline Nguyen, Demo is an proof-of-concept app that's intended for musicians to collaborate on their music remotely. Demo keeps logs of past versions of songs and allows band members to add annotations to sections they want changed. Reflecting back on it now, I'd probably change a few things like larger type size, larger buttons, condensing the first 2 screens into a dropdown folder structure, and more obvious iconography.
