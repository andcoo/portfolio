Neo Blackletter
Typeface / Woodblock Design
2016 / 2017
 

Post-Digital

Although new digital technologies are becoming increasingly prevalent in our lives, we still find ourselves resurrecting older analogue media such as vinyl, film, and audio cassettes, embracing them for their physical qualities and imperfections. This laser-cut woodblock typeface set highlights tensions between the old and new through its design of letterforms and chosen mediums.

 

Past Influences

 

Fette Fraktur Display from the J.B. Metzler’schen Schrift-Schneiderei [Stuttgart, Germany — 1840]

Calligraphy from a Latin Bible of 1407. Written in Belgium by Gerard Brils.

 

Typeface

While combination of laser-cutter and letterpress demonstrate the contrast/unification between digital and analogue technologies, I further communicated that through influence from blackletter type.

 
 

The angle of the strokes are intentionally incorrect, highlighting the typeface's digital inauthenticity while also helping with legibility. In addition to creating the type set, I had to determine the bounding box around each letter to ensure uniform optical spacing.

 
 
 

Woodblock Production

I opted to use masonite to create the woodblock type, which proved to be inexpensive and effective at holding ink. Each block was made of 4 layers of masonite—three layers for the base and the fourth layer for the raised letterform. To ensure proper registration when assembling each woodblock, I used the surrounding masonite as a guide, clamping it to the base layers.

 

Letterpress Printing

The printing process was highly involved, requiring multiple trials to adjust the alignment and pressure of the machine.

—William Morris
