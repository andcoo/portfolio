Broad City
Title Sequence
2017
 

Overview

Broad City chronicles the misadventures of Abby and Alana, two young women struggling to navigate through the chaos of New York City. The original intro is simple, abstract, and changes every episode. While being short and sweet, I felt that it seemed to not communicate any of the themes of the show: feminism, sexuality, or drugs. Using the existing countdown music as a base, I briefly sketched some storyboards.

 
 

Sketches

My first idea was the least concrete. I wanted to create some kind of countdown made up objects that would transform from one number to the next. I wanted that object to represent at least one of the themes, but at the time I wasn’t sure what object would be suitable.

The second idea was a countdown from the perspective of a taxi driving through New York City, narrowly avoiding cars shaped as numbers, eventually crashing to go up to heaven when the Broad City title resided. Through this idea I wanted to communicate a chaotic yet hilarious journey through NYC. Unfortunately I think this concept focused more on cars and NYC rather than the established themes.

 
 
 
 
 

My third Idea focused on drug culture and transformed the countdown into an instructional video on how to roll a joint. This idea seemed to be the strongest to me initially, but then I realized that this would present the show as mainly a stoner comedy whereas marijuana was a smaller component of the show.

Drawing back to the first concept as a basis, I thought the perfect metaphor for feminism and overt sexuality would be shots of dildos flying through New York. This was the route I chose because those themes were the most dominant in the show, and because it felt the most unexpectedly hilarious.

 
 
 

Modelling

Starting with subdivided primitives, I began sculpting a dildo, making sure to include all necessary details. Once I was satisfied with the geometry, I spline-wrapped the model to a bezier curve in the shape of numbers.

 
 
 
 

Lighting & Textures

For each scene, the dildos would alternate between four different 360° panoramas of New York City, each capturing a unique time and place. I experimented with various materials, but ultimately settled on a one with a subtle gradient because it created a fake subsurface-scattering effect (which my laptop normally wouldn’t be able to handle).

 
 
 
 

Final Render

Unfortunately, due to the limited power of my computer, I wasn’t able to render out the final video with high render settings. Below are frames with the render settings cranked up to the level I intended them to be in the video, as well as adjusted bump maps and reflectance.
